Article 3. (Location)

The Company shall have its head office in Seoul and may have branch or office at a suitable place within or outside of Korea, if necessary, by the resolution of the Board of Directors.

Article 4. (Method of public notices)

Public notices of the Company shall be given in “THE JOONG-ANG DAILY NEWS,” a daily newspaper published in Seoul.

Article 5. (Total number of authorized shares)

The total number of authorized shares shall be 500,000,000 shares.

Article 6. (Par value)

The par value per each share to be issued by the Company shall be KRW 100.

Article 7. (Classes of shares and share certificates)

  • ① The shares to be issued by the Company shall be registered common shares and registered preferred shares.
  • ② The preferred shares to be issued by the Company shall be non-voting and the number of such shares to be issued shall be 50,000,000 shares.
  • ③ The rate of preferential dividends per annum payable on preferred shares (the “Preferential Dividends”) shall be not less than 1% per annum of its par value, as determined by the Board of Directors at the time of issuance thereof.
  • ④ In case where the dividends are paid on common shares, preferred shares shall be entitled to receive dividends with common shares in the rate of dividends identical to that of common shares, in addition to the Preferential Dividends.
  • ⑤ If, for any fiscal year, dividends have not been paid on preferred shares at the dividend rate prescribed herein, such unpaid dividends shall be preferentially paid on a cumulative basis at the time of payment of dividends for the subsequent fiscal year.
  • ⑥ In case where no preferred dividend is paid with respect to a fiscal year, preferred shares shall have voting rights from the date of the Shareholders’ Meeting which follows the Shareholders’ Meeting at which a resolution for the non-payment of preferred dividend is made, until the Shareholders’ Meeting at which a resolution for a payment of preferred dividend is made.
  • ⑦ In case of issuance of additional shares for value or for free, or in case of the distribution of stock dividends, the Company shall, in principle, issue common shares for common shares and preferred shares for preferred shares with the same characteristics thereof in proportion to the number of shares being held. However, the Company may issue, if necessary, only one class of shares, in the event of issuance of additional shares for value or the distribution of stock dividends. In such case, all shareholders shall be entitled to subscribe for or receive the shares as above issued.
  • ⑧ Share certificates of the Company shall be issued in eight denominations of one (1), five(5), ten(10), fifty(50), one hundred (100), five hundred (500), one thousand (1,000) and ten thousand (10,000) shares

Article 8. (Transfer agent)

  • ① The Company shall appoint a transfer agent.
  • ② The transfer agent, its place of business and the scope of services to be provided by the transfer agent on behalf of the Company shall be determined by a resolution of the Board of Directors, and shall be notified publicly by the Company.
  • ③ The Company shall have the list of shareholders or a copy thereof kept and maintained at the office of the transfer agent and shall have the transfer agent to deal with the transfer of shares, registration or cancellation of pledges created on shares, registration or cancellation of the property in trust, issuance of share certificates, acceptance of reports and other share-related matters.
  • ④ The procedures for handling the matters provided in Section (3) shall be subject to the Regulations for Transfer Agency of Securities, etc.

Article 9. (Deleted)

Article 10. (Issuance and allocation of shares)

  • ① Issuance of new shares by the Company by a resolution of the Board of Directors shall be in accordance with the following methods:
    • By granting the preemptive rights to subscribe for the new shares to the existing shareholders in proportion to their respective shareholding;
    • To the extent not exceeding 30/100 of the total number of issued and outstanding shares, by granting the preemptive rights to subscribe for the new shares to certain persons by a means other than as prescribed by the Subsection (1) above (including the Company’s existing shareholders) if necessary for improvement of the Company’s financials, introduction of new knowledge, protection of control, and fulfillment of managerial purpose of the Company toward foreign and domestic financial institutions, investors, partners, etc.;
    • To the extent not exceeding 30/100 of the total number of issued and outstanding shares, by granting the preemptive rights to subscribe for the new shares to public (including the Company’s existing shareholders) by a means other than as prescribed by the Subsection (1) above, for the purpose of listing or other financing pursuant to the Financial Investment Services and Capital Markets Act.
  • ② If the shares are issued pursuant to the Section (1) Subsection (3) above, the Company shall issue the new shares as follows by a resolution of the Board of Directors:
    • By offering new shares to public, without setting any criterion for eligibility to subscribe;
    • By offering new shares to members of the Employees Share Ownership Association, and by granting right to subscribe the shares not subscribed by the members of the Employees Share Ownership Association to public;
    • By granting the preemptive rights to subscribe for the new shares to the Company’s existing shareholders, and by granting the rights to subscribe for the new shares to public if there are unsubscribed shares; or
    • By granting the rights to subscribe for the new shares to certain types of persons pursuant to reasonable criterion prescribed by the relevant law, including a forecast of demand by an investment trader of an investment broker as an underwriter or an agent.
  • ③ If new shares are issued under the Section (1) Subsection (2), and (3) above, the Company shall notify the existing shareholders, or give public notice of, the information as required under the Article 416 (1), (2), (2-2), (3), and (4) of the Korean Commercial Code (the “KCC”) two (2) weeks prior to the payment date; provided that, public disclosure of a material fact report with the Financial Services Commission and the Exchange pursuant to Article 165-9 of the Financial Investment Services and Capital Markets Act may be made in lieu of the notification and public notice above.
  • ④ If shares are issued under each Subsection of Section (1) above, the class, number and issue price of the shares shall be determined by a resolution of the Board of Directors.
  • ⑤ In case where the Company issues new shares and there are shares not subscribed for or paid for, the terms of their disposition such as the appropriate issue price shall be determined by a resolution of the Board of Directors pursuant to the relevant laws.
  • ⑥ In case where the Company issues new shares and there are fractional shares, the terms of their disposition shall be determined by a resolution of the Board of Directors.
  • ⑦ If new shares are issued under the Section (1) Subsection (1), the Company shall issue the preemptive right certificates to the shareholders.

Article 11. (Commencement date for dividends on new shares)

With regard to payment of dividends on the new shares issued by the Company as a result of issuance of new shares or bonus shares, or stock dividends, such new shares shall be deemed to have been issued at the end of the fiscal year immediately preceding the fiscal year to which the time of issuance thereof belongs; provided that with regard to the payment of interim dividends on new shares issued by the Company as a result of issuance of new shares or bonus shares, or stock dividends on or after the record date of interim dividend of the Article 39-2(1), the new shares shall be deemed to have been issued immediately after the record date of interim dividend.

Article 11 – 2 (Stock Option)

  • ① The Company may grant the stock options to its executives and employees (including executives and employees of affiliated company stipulated in Article 30 of the Enforcement Decree of the KCC. This definition applies the same in this Article) to the extent not exceeding 15/100 of the total number of issued and outstanding shares by a special resolution of the General Shareholders’ Meeting; provided however that the Company may grant the stock options to persons other than the directors of the Company to the extent not exceeding 3/100 of the total number of issued and outstanding shares by a resolution of the Board of Directors. If the Company grants the stock options by a resolution of the Board of Directors, the Company shall obtain the approval of a General Shareholders’ Meeting convened for the first time following such a resolution. The stock options granted by a resolution of the General Shareholders’ Meeting or the Board of Directors may be linked to the performance of the Company measured by targeted managerial results or market indices.
  • ② Those who are eligible for a stock option shall be the Company’s executives and employees who contribute to or are capable of contributing to the Company’s incorporation or management, or technological innovation etc., ; provided however that the executives and employees who are not eligible for the stock options under applicable laws and regulations shall be excluded.
  • ③ The shares to be delivered as a result of the exercise of stock options hereunder (or, if the difference between the share price at which such stock options are exercised and the market value of the relevant share is paid in cash or treasury share, the shares on the basis of which such difference is calculated) shall be determined among the shares mentioned in Article 7, by a resolution of the General Shareholders’ Meeting or the Board of Directors granting the stock option.
  • ④ The per-share price at which stock options are exercised shall not be lower than either of the following prices:
    • If the new shares are to be issued and delivered, the higher of the following prices:
      • i.The fair market value of the relevant shares as of the date on which the stock option is granted; or
      • ii.The face value of the shares.
    • If the treasury shares are delivered, , the fair market value the relevant shares as of the date on which the stock option is granted.
  • ⑤ The stock option may be exercised until the date determined by a resolution of the General Shareholders’ Meeting or the Board of Directors, for eight (8) years after the second anniversary of the date of the resolution of the General Shareholders’ Meeting or the Board of Directors of the Section (1) above.
  • ⑥ A person who is granted a stock option is entitled to exercise the stock option only if such person has been in office in the Company or employed by the Company at least for two (2) years from the date of the resolution mentioned in Subsection (1) above; provided, however, that, if the said grantee dies or resigns from the Company within two (2) years from the date of the resolution mentioned in Subsection (1) above due to a reason not attributable to such person, the stock option may be exercised within the period originally set for exercising the same.
  • ⑦ The terms and conditions of the stock option, such as the contents and exercise price thereof, shall be determined by a special resolution of the General Shareholders’ Meeting or a resolution of the Board of Directors in accordance with applicable laws and regulations provided, however, that the matters which are not provided for as matters reserved for resolutions of the General Shareholders’ Meeting under applicable laws, regulations and the Articles of Incorporation may be determined by the Board of Directors.
  • ⑧ The Company may cancel a grant of stock option by a resolution of the Board of Directors if:
    • an executive or employee of the Company who has been granted a stock option voluntarily retires or resign;
    • an executive or employee of the Company who has been granted a stock option has caused material damages to the Company by willful acts or negligence;
    • the Company is unable to respond to the exercise of such a stock option due to the Company’s bankruptcy, dissolution, or otherwise; or
    • There has occurred any event constituting a cause of cancellation thereof as provided in relevant stock option agreement.
  • ⑨ Article 11 hereof shall apply mutatis mutandis with respect to the payment of dividends on the shares issued as a result of the exercise of the stock options hereunder.

Article 12. (Deleted)

Article 13. (Report of addresses, names and seal impression or signature of shareholders)

  • ① All shareholders and registered pledgees shall report to the transfer agent referred to in Article 8 their names, addresses and seal impressions or signature, etc.
  • ② Each of the shareholders and registered pledgees residing outside Korea shall report to the transfer agent his/her provisional address to which and his/her agent to whom notices may be given by the Company within Korea.
  • ③ In case of any change in the information mentioned in preceding Section (1) and (2), the same shall apply.

Article 14. (Suspension of altering entry in the list of shareholders and Record Date)

  • ① The Company shall suspend transfer of shares, registration or cancellation of pledges and declaration or cancellation of property under trust within one (1) month from the day immediately following the last day of each settlement period.
  • ② The Company shall deem those shareholders whose name appear in the list of shareholders as of the last day of each settlement period to be the shareholders who are entitled to exercise their rights as shareholders at the General Shareholders’ Meeting to be convened in respect of the said settlement period.
  • ③ The Company may suspend entry of alterations in the list of shareholders with respect to shareholders’ rights for a given period not exceeding three (3) months by a resolution of the Board of Directors with two (2) weeks’ prior public notice, if necessary for a special meeting of shareholders or otherwise. The Board of Directors may designate such a record date, together with suspension of altering entry in the list of shareholders, if the Board of Directors deems it necessary.

Article 15. (Convening and notice of meeting)

  • ① The Company shall convene the ordinary General Shareholders’ Meeting within three (3) months after the closure of each fiscal year and may convene an extraordinary General Shareholders’ Meeting when necessary.
  • ② Unless otherwise provided in relevant laws and regulations, the General Shareholders’ Meeting of the Section (1) shall be convened by the Representative Director by a resolution of the Board of Directors.
  • ③ In convening the General Shareholders’ Meeting, the Company shall give notice in writing to each shareholder of the date, time and place of the meeting and the list of agenda to be dealt with at the meeting, at least two (2) weeks prior to the date set for such meeting.
  • ④ For shareholders holding one percent (1%) or less of the total number of issued and outstanding shares with voting rights, the Company may insert twice or more in the "JOONG-ANG DAILY NEWS and the KOREA ECONOMIC DAILY" published in Seoul or give public notice on the electronic disclosure system operated by Financial Supervisory Service or Korea Exchange, of its intention to convene such a meeting and the list of agenda to be dealt with at the meeting two weeks in advance of the meeting, in lieu of the notice set forth in Subsection (3) above.

Article 16. (Place of convening the General Shareholders’ Meeting)

The General Shareholders’ Meeting shall be held in the place where the head office of the Company is located or any other place adjacent to it.

Article 17. (Chairman)

  • ① The Representative Director shall preside at the General Shareholders’ Meeting as chairman. In case there are more than one Representative Director, the Board of Directors shall determine the chairman of the General Shareholders’ Meeting.
  • ② If the Representative Director is unable to perform his or her duty, the duty to serve as the chairman of the General Shareholders’ Meeting shall be performed in the order as determined by the Board of Directors.

Article 17 – 2 (Chairman’s authority to maintenance order)

  • ① The chairman of a General Shareholders’ Meeting may stop a person who significantly disturbs order in such a meeting (including with speech or behavior to interfere with the proceedings of the meeting intentionally) from speaking or may order such a person out of the meeting.
  • ② The chairman of a General Shareholders’ Meeting may restrict the duration and/or the number of times of speech by each shareholder, whenever the chairman deems it necessary for the purpose of smooth proceeding of the meeting.

Article 18. (Shareholders’ voting right)

Unless otherwise provided for by law, each shareholder shall have one (1) vote for each share owned.

Article 19. (Method of adopting resolutions)

Unless otherwise provided in the relevant laws and regulations, all resolutions of the meetings of shareholders shall be passed by the affirmative votes of a majority of the shares represented by the shareholders present at the General Shareholders’ Meeting, which shall not be less than a quarter of the total number of issued and outstanding shares of the Company.

Article 20. (Voting by proxy)

  • ① Each shareholder may exercise his/her vote by a proxy. The proxy shall present to the Company a power of attorney evidencing his/her power of representation prior to opening of the General Shareholders’ Meeting.
  • ② In case the legal representative of a shareholder grants power of attorney to another shareholder under the preceding section, a document evidencing the power of legal representation shall also submitted.

Article 21. (Minutes of the General Shareholders’ Meeting)

The proceedings and results of the General Shareholders’ Meeting shall be recorded in the minutes and the chairman and directors present shall affix their seals or sign thereon.

Article 22. (Number of Directors)

  • ① The Company shall have not less than three (3) directors, but not more than fourteen (14) directors and the number of the outside directors shall be three (3) or more and majority of the total number of directors. Directors shall be elected by the General Shareholders’ Meeting.
  • ② If there is a vacancy in the number of directors, election to fill a vacancy may be postponed until the next Ordinary General Shareholders’ Meeting, as long as the number of the existing directors in office is not less than the number of directors prescribed by law and no hindrance is caused to carrying on the Company’s business thereby.

Article 23. (Election of directors)

  • ① A resolution for electing directors shall be passed by the affirmative votes of a majority of the shares represented by the shareholders present at the General Shareholders’ Meeting, which shall not be less than a quarter of the total number of issued and outstanding shares.
  • ② In case two (2) directors or more are elected at the General Shareholders’ Meeting, the cumulative vote stipulated in Article 382-2 of the KCC shall not apply.
  • ③ An outside director shall have professional background in management, economics, accounting, law or engineering and many experiences in his field and be fully qualified according to the relevant regulation. Persons who are or were within recent two (2) years executives or employees of the Company or its affiliate (as defined under the regulations under the Monopoly Regulation and Fair Trade Act (the “MRFTA”), or the major shareholders of the Company and their blood relatives, shall be excluded.

Article 24. (Term of office)

  • ① The term of office of directors shall be three (3) years; provided however that, such term of office shall be extended until the close of the annual meeting of shareholders convened in respect of the last period for the settlement of accounts comprised in their term of office if their term of office expires after the end of the said last period for the settlement of accounts but before the close of the said General Shareholders’ Meeting.
  • ② The term of office of a director who is elected to fill a vacancy shall be the remaining term of his or her predecessor.

Article 25. (Duties of directors)

  • ① The Representative Director shall represent the Company in accordance with the law and shall execute the matters decided by the Board of Directors and shall control all the affairs of the Company.
  • ② Directors shall assist the Representative Director and their responsibilities will be determined according to the Board of Directors. If the Representative Director is unable to perform his duty, a director shall perform his duties as determined by the Board of Directors.
  • ③ If a director finds anything that is likely to cause material damages to the Company, he/she shall immediately report the same to the Audit Committee.
  • ④ A director shall report the performance of his or her duties to the Board of Directors once or more in three (3) month.

Article 25 – 2. (Duty of Confidentiality of Directors)

During and after the term of office, a director shall not disclose the trade secrets of the Company he/she came to knowledge.

Article 26. (Appointment of the Representative Director)

The Company shall appoint a Representative Director among its directors by a resolution of the Board of Directors and the Representative Directors shall represent the Company. The Company shall also appoint a number of Chairman, Vice Chairman, Presidents, Vice Presidents, Senior Managing Directors and Managing Directors.

Article 27. (The Board of Directors)

  • ① The Board of Directors shall determine all business operation, to the extent that they are not reserved for a resolution by the General Shareholders’ Meeting.
  • ② The proceedings of the Board of Directors shall be recorded in minutes, which shall include agenda, proceedings, results of the proceedings of the meeting, names of the directors against each resolution and the reason for their objection thereto and the chairman and all directors present at the meeting shall sign and seal the same or affix their signatures thereto.

Article 28. (Chairman of the Board of Directors)

The Director appointed among its directors by a resolution of the Board of Directors shall be the chairman of the Board of Directors.

Article 28 – 2 (Committees)

  • ① The Company may establish the following committees in the Board of Directors by a resolution of the Board of Directors:
    • Management Committee;
    • Committee for Recommending Candidates for Outside Directors;
    • Audit Committee;
    • Internal Transaction Committee;
    • Compensation Committee;
    • Other committee as deemed necessary by the Board of Directors
  • ② Unless provided otherwise in the relevant laws and regulations, the details concerning the composition, power and operation of each of such committees shall be determined by resolutions of the Board of Directors.
  • ③ Articles 30, 27(2) and 29 hereof shall apply mutatis mutandis with respect to the committee.
  • ④ The Board of Directors shall supervise the activities of the committees, and its responsibilities shall not diminish as a result of delegating such responsibilities to the Management Committee.

Article 28 – 3 (Management Committee)

  • ① Management Committee shall perform duties as determined by the regulations and resolutions of the Board of Directors, and shall review and determine matters delegated by the Board of Directors time to time.
  • ② Details concerning composition, operation, etc. of the Management Committee shall be determined by the Board of Directors.

Article 28 – 4 (Audit Committee)

  • ① The Company shall establish an Audit Committee as set forth in Article 28 – 2 in lieu of the Auditor.
  • ② Audit Committee shall be composed of at least three (3) directors. 2/3 or more of the members of the Audit Committee shall be the outside directors and the members of Audit Committee who are not outside directors shall be duly qualified under the Article 542-10(2) of the KCC.
  • ③ If the aggregate of the voting shares held by a shareholder exceeds 3/100 of the total number of issued and outstanding voting shares, such shareholder shall not be allowed to exercise his/her votes with respect to the shares in excess of the above limit, in the election of the member of Audit Committee who is an outside director
  • ④ If the aggregate of the voting shares held by the major shareholder, his/her specially-related person, the person who holds the shares on the account of such major shareholder and his/her specially related person, and the person who has entrusted his voting rights to such largest shareholder and his specially related person, exceeds 3/100 of the total number of issued and outstanding voting shares, such shareholder shall not be allowed to exercise his votes with respect to the shares in excess of the above limit, in the election and retirement of the member of Audit Committee who is not an outside director.
  • ⑤ Audit Committee shall, by its resolution, appoint a person representing Audit Committee. The Chair of the Committee shall be an outside director.

Article 28 – 5 (Duties of Audit Committee)

  • ① The Audit Committee shall audit the Company’s accounting and general operations.
  • ② The Audit Committee may request a director (a person who is authorized to convene the meeting of the Board of Directors, the same shall apply hereinafter) to convene a The Board of Directors’ Meeting by submitting a written request stating the business to be dealt with at the proposed meeting and the reason for convening such a meeting.
  • ③ If the director fails promptly to convene the meeting of the Board of Directors upon the request set forth in Subsection (2) above, the Audit Committee who made the request may convene the Board of Directors’ Meeting.
  • ④ The Audit Committee may request the Board of Directors to convene an Extraordinary General Shareholders Meeting in writing, stating the agenda to be dealt with at such meeting and the reason for convening such meeting.
  • ⑤ The Audit Committee may request the Company’s subsidiary(s) to make a report on its (their) operations, if the Audit Committee deems it necessary to perform its duties. In such a case, if the subsidiary(s) fails to immediately make such a report as requested or the Audit Committee deems it necessary to verify the content of the report made by the subsidiary(s), the Audit Committee shall have the right to inspect that subsidiary’s operations and status of assets.
  • ⑥ In electing an independent auditor, the Audit Committee shall approve such an independent auditor duly elected.
  • ⑦ In addition to the matters in Subsection (1) to (6) above, the Audit Committee shall deal with the matters delegated to it by the Board of Directors.
  • ⑧ The Board of Directors may not make a resolution with respect to Audit Committee’s resolution.
  • ⑨ Audit Committee may use professional assistance at the expense of the Company.

Article 28 – 6 (Minutes of Audit)

The Audit Committee shall prepare minutes of audit with respect to the audit conducted by it. The minutes shall include the method of audit and the results thereof, and shall be signed and sealed by or shall bear the signatures of, the Audit Committee members who have conducted such audit.

Article 28 – 7 (Other Provisions)

Article 22(2) shall apply mutatis mutandis with respect to a member of Audit Committee.

Article 29. (Convening of the Board of Directors’ Meeting)

  • ① The chairman shall convene the Board of Directors’ meeting by giving notice thereof to each director twenty in writing, through electronic documents, or in person, at least twenty four (24) hours prior to the date set for each of such meetings; provided, however, that, if all directors unanimously consent to holding the Board of Directors’ meeting, the procedure for convening a meeting may be omitted.
  • ② Each director may convene the Board of Directors’ meeting with the consent of the chairman when deemed necessary for the performance of the business. In such case, the preceding Subsection shall apply mutatis mutandis.

Article 30. (Method of Adopting Resolution of the Board of Directors)

  • ① A quorum for holding a meeting of the Board of Directors shall be a majority of all directors in office and all resolutions of the Board of Directors shall be adopted by the affirmative votes of a majority of directors present at the meeting.
  • ② The Board of Directors may allow all directors or a part thereof to participate in resolutions of the Board of Directors through the means of communication transmitting and receiving voices simultaneously, in lieu of attending such a meeting in person. In such case, such directors shall be deemed to have attended such a meeting in person.
  • ③ No directors having a specific interest in any resolution of the Board of Directors shall be allowed to exercise their vote upon such a resolution.

Article 31. (Non-Competition of Director)

A director shall not conduct the same kind of business as the Company without consent of the Board of Directors, except when such director is appointed with the knowledge that his business is in competition with the Company.

Article 35. (Remuneration and retirement benefit of directors)

  • ① The ceiling amount of remuneration for the directors shall be determined by a resolution of the General Shareholders’ Meeting.
  • ② The retirement benefit for the directors shall be paid in accordance with the separate internal rules for retirement benefits of the executives.

Article 37. (Fiscal year and settlement of account)

The fiscal year of the Company shall commence on January 1 and end on December 31 of each year and the settlement of accounts shall be made once a year, as of the last day of December of each year. However, the first fiscal year following the establishment shall end on the last day of December of the following year.

Article 38. (Preparation and maintenance of financial statements and business report)

  • ① The Representative Director of the Company shall prepare and submit to the Audit Committee for audit the following documents and their supplementary schedules together with an business report, six (6) weeks prior to the date set for the annual meeting of shareholders convened for the fiscal year to which such documents are related, and shall submit the aforementioned documents and the business report to the Ordinary General Shareholders’ Meeting:
    • Balance Sheet;
    • Income Statement;
    • Other documents which display the Company’s financial status and management performance, as designated by the Enforcement Decree of the KCC.
  • ② If the Company is required to prepare consolidated financial statements under the Enforcement Decree of the KCC, such consolidated financial statements shall be prepared in addition to the financial statements under Section (1) above.
  • ③ The Audit Committee shall submit to the Representative Director the audit report by one (1) week prior to the General Shareholders’ Meeting.
  • ④ Representative Director of the Company shall keep the documents referred to in Section (1), together with the business report and audit report at the main office for 5 years from one (1) week prior to the date of the Ordinary General Shareholders’ Meeting, and their certified copies at branches for 3 years.
  • ⑤ Upon the approval of the documents referred to in Section (1) at the General Shareholders’ Meeting, Representative Director of the Company shall publically disclose without delay the balance sheet and the opinion of the independent auditor.
  • ⑥ Notwithstanding the Section (1) above, the Board of Directors may approve by a resolution if each of the following is satisfied:
    • If the independent auditor provides a favorable opinion that each of the itemized statements and the business reports of the Section (1) accurately represent the financials and business performance of the Company; and
    • All members of the Audit Committee unanimously consent.
  • ⑦ If the Board of Directors approves pursuant to the Section (6), the Representative Director shall report the contents of each of the itemized statements from Section (1) to the General Shareholders’ Meeting. In this case, an approval by the General Shareholders’ Meeting shall mean an approval by the Board of Directors.

Article 38 – 2 (Disposition of Profit)

The Company shall dispose of the unappropriated retained earnings of each fiscal year in the following order of priority:

  • Earned profit reserve (earned profit reserve under the KCC);
  • Other statutory reserve;
  • Dividends;
  • Voluntary reserve;
  • Other disposition of earned surplus;
  • Earned surplus carried over to the next term.

Article 39. (Dividends)

  • ① Dividends may be paid in cash or in shares.
  • ② Dividends referred to in Section (1) above shall be paid to shareholders or registered pledgees whose name appear or are duly registered in the list of shareholders as of the end of each fiscal year.

Article 39 – 2. (Interim dividends)

  • ① The Company may pay interim dividends under Article 462-3 of the KCC to its shareholders whose names appear in the list of shareholders as of 00:00, June 30.
  • ② Interim dividends referred to in Section (1) above shall be paid by a resolution of the Board of Directors; provided, however, that such a resolution shall be made within 45 days from the record date specified in Section (1) above.
  • ③ Interim dividends shall be paid within the limit of not exceeding the amount of the net worth shown on the balance sheet as of the end of the immediately preceding period for the settlement of accounts less the amount of the following items:
    • The amount of capital, as of the end of the immediately preceding period for the settlement of accounts;
    • The aggregate sum of the capital reserves and legal reserves appropriated up to the immediately preceding period for the settlement of accounts;
    • Unearned income as defined under the Enforcement Decree of the Commercial Code;
    • The amount appropriated for dividends by a resolution adopted at the General Shareholders’ Meeting convened for the immediately preceding period for the settlement of accounts;
    • The amount of voluntary reserves to be appropriated for a specific purpose as pursuant to the Articles of Incorporation or a resolution by the Shareholders’ Meeting; and
    • The amount of legal reserves to be appropriated for the current period for the settlement of accounts, as a result of such interim dividends.
  • ④ If any new shares have been issued prior to the respective record dates specified in Section (1) above following the commencement date of the current fiscal year (including as a result of capitalization of reserves, stock dividends, requests for conversion of convertible bonds to the capital shares and the exercise of warrant with respect to bonds with warrant), such new shares shall be deemed to have been issued at the end of the immediately preceding fiscal year with respect to interim dividends hereunder.

Article 40. (Statute of Limitation to the Claim for Dividends)

  • ① If a claim for dividends has not been exercised for five(5) years, the statute of limitation applicable thereto shall expire.
  • ② The dividends with respect to which the statute of limitation has expired shall become vested in the Company.

Article 41. (Issuance of bonds)

  • ① The Company may issue bonds by a resolution of the Board of Directors.
  • ② The Board of Directors may authorize the Representative Director to issue bonds, for a period not exceeding one (1) year, by prescribing for the amount and type of the bonds to be issued.

Article 41 – 2 (Issuance of convertible bonds)

  • ① The allocation of the convertible bonds issued by the Company to the shareholders shall be carried out pursuant to the Article 7(7) in proportion to the number of shares held by the shareholders. If a shareholder loses or waivers his or her subscription right or if odd lots occur after allocation, the disposition of such bonds shall be determined by the Board of Directors, provided, however, the Company may issue convertible bonds to a person other than existing shareholders in any of the following cases:
    • To the extent that the total face value of the bonds does not exceed KRW 850 billion, issuance of convertible bonds by offering opportunities to subscribe the bonds to certain persons (including the Company’s existing shareholders) in furtherance of the Company’s business purposes, including introduction of new technology, improvement of the Company’s financials, emergency financing, issuance of foreign convertible bonds, strategic alliances, etc. by a means other than as prescribed by the Article 10(1)1.
    • To the extent that the total face value of the bonds does not exceed KRW 850 billion, issuance of convertible bonds by offering opportunities to subscribe the bonds to public (including the Company’s existing shareholders) by a means other than as prescribed by the Article 10(1)1.
  • ② If the bonds are issued pursuant to the Section (1) Subsection (2) above, the allocation of such bonds shall be in accordance with the following methods by a resolution of the Board of Directors:
    • By offering bonds to public, without setting any criterion for eligibility to subscribe;;
    • By granting the rights to subscribe the bonds to the shareholders and by granting right to subscribe the shares not subscribed by the shareholders to public;; or
    • By granting the rights to subscribe for the bonds to certain types of persons pursuant to reasonable criterion prescribed by the relevant law, including a forecast of demand by an investment trader of an investment broker as an underwriter or an agent.
  • ③ The Board of Directors may determine that the convertible bonds referred to in Section (1) may be issued on the condition that only a portion of the convertible bonds shall have conversion rights.
  • ④ The shares to be issued as a result of the conversion of such bonds shall be common shares for KRW 630 billion and preferred shares pursuant to Article 7 for KRW 220 billion of the total face value of the issued convertible bonds and the applicable conversion price shall be equal to or higher than the par value per share of such new shares, as determined by the Board of Directors at the time of issuance of such bonds.
  • ⑤ The period in which holders of convertible bonds are entitled to make a request for conversion hereunder shall be from the day of the date of issuance thereof and end on the day immediately preceding the maturity date thereof provided, however, that the period for requesting conversion may be adjusted by a resolution of the Board of Directors within the aforementioned period.
  • ⑥ In the case of any distribution of dividends or interest on the shares issued upon conversion under Section (1), the convertible bonds shall be deemed to be converted into shares at the end of the fiscal year immediately preceding the fiscal year during which the relevant conversion rights are exercised.
  • ⑦ With respect to the interim dividend on new shares issued upon exercise of the pre-emptive right after the record date for interim dividend under Article 39-2(1), the new shares shall be deemed to be issued immediately after the record date for interim dividend.

Article 41 – 3 (Issuance of bonds with warrant)

  • ① The allocation of the bonds with warrant issued by the Company to the shareholders shall be carried out pursuant to the Article 7(7) in proportion to the number of shares held by the shareholders. If a shareholder loses or waivers his or her subscription right or if odd lots occur after allocation, the disposition of such bonds shall be determined by the Board of Directors, provided, however, the Company may issue bonds with warrant to a person other than existing shareholders in any of the following cases:
    • To the extent that the total face value of the bonds does not exceed KRW 850 billion, issuance of bonds with warrant by offering of opportunities to subscribe the bonds to certain persons (including the Company’s existing shareholders) in furtherance of the Company’s business purposes, including introduction of new technology, improvement of the Company’s financials, emergency financing, issuance of foreign bonds with warrant, strategic alliances, etc. by a means other than as prescribed by the Article 10(1)1.
    • To the extent that the total face value of the bonds does not exceed KRW 850 billion, issuance of bonds with warrant by offering opportunities to subscribe the bonds to certain persons (including the Company’s existing shareholders) in furtherance of the Company’s business purposes, including introduction of new technology, improvement of the Company’s financials, emergency financing, issuance of foreign bonds with warrant, strategic alliances, etc. by a means other than as prescribed by the Article 10(1)1.
  • ② If the bonds are issued pursuant to the Section (1) Subsection (2) above, the allocation of such bonds shall be in accordance with the following methods by a resolution of the Board of Directors:
    • By offering bonds to public, without setting any criterion for eligibility to subscribe;
    • By granting the rights to subscribe the bonds to the shareholders and by granting right to subscribe the shares not subscribed by the shareholders to public; or
    • By granting the rights to subscribe for the bonds to certain types of persons pursuant to reasonable criterion prescribed by the relevant law, including a forecast of demand by an investment trader of an investment broker as an underwriter or an agent.
  • ③ The amount of new shares which can be subscribed for by the holders of the bonds with warrants shall be determined by the Board of Directors; provided that the maximum amount of such new shares shall not exceed the aggregate face value of the bonds with warrants.
  • ④ The shares to be issued as a result of the conversion of the bonds with warrant shall be common shares for KRW 630 billion and preferred shares pursuant to Article 7 for KRW 220 billion of the total face value of the issued bonds with warrant and the applicable conversion price shall be equal to or higher than the par value per share of such new shares, as determined by the Board of Directors at the time of issuance of such bonds
  • ⑤ The period in which holders of convertible bonds are entitled to make a request for conversion hereunder shall be from the day of the date of issuance thereof and end on the day immediately preceding the maturity date thereof provided, however, that the period for requesting conversion may be adjusted by a resolution of the Board of Directors within the aforementioned period.
  • ⑥ In the case of distribution of dividends or interest on the shares issued upon exercise of warrants under the Section (1), such shares shall be deemed to be issued at the end of the fiscal year immediately preceding the fiscal year during which the relevant subscription money is fully paid.
  • ⑦ With respect to the interim dividend on new shares issued upon exercise of the pre-emptive right after the record date for interim dividend under Article 39-2(1), the new shares shall be deemed to be issued immediately after the record date for interim dividend.

Article 42. (Approval of internal transactions)

  • ① The Company shall obtain approval from the Board of Directors in order to enter into large scale internal transactions as prescribed in Article 11-2 of the MRFTA with the Specially Related Party as defined under the MRFTA.
  • ② The Board of Directors shall consider the intent of the relevant laws and make rational business judgment when it approves the transaction under the Section (1).
  • ③ The Company shall obtain approval from the Board of Directors in order to enter into transactions as prescribed in Article 542-9 of The KCC with the Company’s largest shareholder (including his/her specially related person as under Article 542-9 of The KCC) or his/her specially related person as defined under the KCC.
  • ④ The Company shall obtain approval from the Board of Directors in case of entering into transactions with the Company’s largest shareholders and their specially related person, or Company’s specially related person in accordance with Section (3).
